About

Background & Expertise

I am a researcher and educator based in Dhaka, Bangladesh, working at the intersection of machine learning, natural language processing, and human-centered computing. My work is driven by one core question: how do we build AI systems that people can actually understand, trust, and hold accountable?

I combine rigorous technical ML work with empirical human-centered methods — developing predictive models, running controlled experiments, and conducting qualitative studies. My research spans health, misinformation, and responsible AI, and has appeared in JMIR, ACM CHI, ACM UbiComp, ACM JCSS, IEEE, Springer, and Oxford University Press.

Alongside research, I serve as Senior Lab Instructor at NSU's ECE Department — having mentored over 1,000 students across 10+ semesters — and as Adjunct Lecturer in CSE at Independent University, Bangladesh.

"My goal is to make AI systems that are not just accurate, but interpretable, accountable, and genuinely useful for the people who need them most."

Research

Project Areas

AI in Healthcare · Machine Learning
Explainable AI for mHealth & Clinical Decision-Making
Sequential mixed-methods study (n=137 survey + 20 qualitative interviews) examining explainability needs across clinicians, XAI experts, and developers in AI-driven mHealth. Produced a five-pillar XAI framework spanning clinical alignment, contextual personalization, cultural relevance, trust, and usability. Ongoing: controlled LLM experiment on health risk decisions and over-reliance.
JMIR (Q1, IF 6.0) — Participatory XAI Framework for mHealth (In Press)
ACM UbiComp/ISWC '24 — Know Your Users: XAI in Bangladesh
MSc Thesis, IUB 2025 — Human-Centered XAI for mHealth
Explainable AI mHealth Mixed Methods LLMs
Applied AI Ethics & Governance · Fairness & Equity
AI Accountability & Ethics Across South Asia
Co-led multi-stakeholder qualitative research with 36 interviews across Bangladesh, India, Pakistan, and Nepal — mapping AI accountability gaps, governance challenges, and ethics principles. Covers generative AI regulation, CS ethics education, and regional AI policy. Secured grant funding and produced top-tier outputs including ACM CHI, ICTD, and Oxford University Press.

ML for Misinformation, Clinical Risk & Robotics
Three applied ML projects united by a focus on building and critically evaluating classifiers: one of the first Bengali fake news datasets (85% Random Forest accuracy), an interpretable fuzzy-logic stroke predictor preferred by clinicians over black-box models, and an AI rescue robot with real-time human detection.

Real-Time Flood Warning System for Rural Bangladesh
Designed and deployed a low-cost IoT sensor network for real-time water level monitoring and mobile-based flood alerting in flash flood-prone communities of North-Eastern Bangladesh. Grounded in community co-design, the system demonstrates how accessible technology can address climate vulnerability and serve populations with limited infrastructure.
IJSWCC 2021 — Development & Testing of the Flood Warning System
